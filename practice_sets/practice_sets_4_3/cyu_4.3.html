Check Your Understanding 4.3

Formative check focused on Unit 2.3: function composition using equations, tables, graphs, order of operations, and domain reasoning.
1.

Let \(f(x)=2x+3\) and \(g(x)=x^2-1\). Evaluate:

  1. \(f(g(2))\)
  2. \(g(f(2))\)
  3. Are the answers the same?
2.

Use the table.

\(x\)\(-2\)\(-1\)\(0\)\(1\)\(2\)
\(f(x)\)\(1\)\(3\)\(2\)\(-1\)\(0\)
\(g(x)\)\(2\)\(0\)\(-1\)\(3\)\(1\)
  1. \(f(g(0))\)
  2. \(g(f(2))\)
  3. \(f(f(1))\)
3.

Let \(f(x)=x^2-4\) and \(g(x)=x+1\).

  1. Find \(f(g(x))\).
  2. Find \(g(f(x))\).
  3. Compare the two results.
4.

Let \(f(x)=\sqrt{x}\) and \(g(x)=x-5\).

  1. Find \(f(g(x))\).
  2. State the domain of \(f(g(x))\).
  3. Explain why the domain is restricted.
5.

Use the table.

\(x\)\(-2\)\(-1\)\(0\)\(1\)\(2\)
\(f(x)\)\(0\)\(1\)\(2\)\(3\)\(4\)
\(g(x)\)\(2\)\(-2\)\(1\)\(0\)\(-1\)
  1. Find \(f(g(-2))\).
  2. Find \(g(f(-2))\).
  3. Explain why one composition may be undefined if the output is not in the table.
6.

Let \(f(x)=x^2\) and \(g(x)=x+3\).

  1. Find \(f(g(x))\).
  2. Find \(g(f(x))\).
  3. Explain why the two expressions are not equivalent.
  4. Describe how the graphs of the two compositions would differ.
7.

Compare function arithmetic and function composition.

Use examples to explain the difference between \((f+g)(x)\) and \(f(g(x))\).

Your explanation should include meaning, notation, and order.

8.

Create two functions \(f\) and \(g\) so that \(f(g(x))=x^2+6x+9\).

  1. Write \(f(x)\) and \(g(x)\).
  2. Verify your composition.
  3. Explain how you designed the functions.
