Unit 4.2 — Sum, Difference, and Pythagorean Identities

Evaluate exactly: \[\cos\left(\frac{\pi}{4}-\frac{\pi}{6}\right)\]

2.

Use sum/difference identities to evaluate \(\sin\left(\frac{5\pi}{12}\right)\).

3.

A student simplifies \(\sin^2(x)+\cos^2(x)\) as \((\sin(x)+\cos(x))^2\).

  1. Explain the mistake.
  2. Expand the incorrect expression.
  3. Compare the two results.
4.

Create a trigonometric identity that simplifies to \(1\). Your identity must use at least two of the following: reciprocal identities, quotient identities, or Pythagorean identities. Then verify it line by line and explain why each rewrite is valid.

Unit 2.2 — Formative Spiral: Function Arithmetic
5.

Let \( f(x)=3x-2 \) and \( g(x)=x+5 \).

  1. Find \((f+g)(x)\).
  2. Find \((f-g)(x)\).
6.

Let \( f(x)=x^2 \) and \( g(x)=2x \).

  1. Evaluate \((fg)(3)\).
  2. Evaluate \(\left(\frac{f}{g}\right)(4)\).
7.

Use the table.

\(x\)\(-1\)\(0\)\(2\)
\(f(x)\)\(3\)\(1\)\(5\)
\(g(x)\)\(2\)\(-4\)\(1\)
  1. Find \((f+g)(0)\).
  2. Find \((fg)(2)\).
8.

State the domain restriction for:

\(\frac{x+1}{x-7}\)

9.

Given:

\(f(x)=\sqrt{x+4}\qquad g(x)=\frac{1}{x-2}\)

Determine the domain of:

  • \(f+g\)
  • \(fg\)

Explain your reasoning.

10.

Given:

\(f(x)=x^2-9\qquad g(x)=x-3\)

  1. Simplify \(\frac{f(x)}{g(x)}\).
  2. Explain why the domain restriction still exists after simplification.
11.

Explain the difference between simplifying a quotient of functions and changing the function itself.

Use \(\frac{x^2-1}{x-1}\) as your example. Mention excluded values, holes, or domain restrictions.

12.

A student argues that function arithmetic is “just combining equations,” so domain does not need to be checked separately.

  1. Critique the student’s argument.
  2. Give at least two examples that show why domain matters.
  3. Write a corrected explanation that could be used as a class note.

Check Your Understanding 4.2

Formative check focused on Unit 2.2: function arithmetic, tables, graphs, domain restrictions, and contextual interpretation.
1.

Let \(f(x)=2x+3\) and \(g(x)=x^2-4\).

  1. Find \((f+g)(x)\).
  2. Find \((f-g)(x)\).
  3. Find \((fg)(x)\).
2.

The graphs of \(f\) and \(g\) are shown.

  1. Estimate \(f(2)\) and \(g(2)\).
  2. Estimate \((f+g)(2)\).
  3. Estimate \((f-g)(2)\).
3.

Let \(f(x)=x^2-1\) and \(g(x)=2x+5\).

  1. Find \((f+g)(x)\).
  2. Find \((f-g)(x)\).
  3. Find \((fg)(x)\).
  4. Find \(\left(\frac{f}{g}\right)(x)\) and state its domain restriction.
4.

Use the table to compare function operations.

\(x\)\(-1\)\(0\)\(1\)\(2\)
\(f(x)\)\(4\)\(3\)\(0\)\(-2\)
\(g(x)\)\(2\)\(-1\)\(5\)\(0\)
  1. Find \((f+g)(1)\).
  2. Find \((fg)(0)\).
  3. Find \(\left(\frac{f}{g}\right)(-1)\).
  4. Explain why \(\left(\frac{f}{g}\right)(2)\) is undefined.
5.

A student simplified \(\frac{x^2-9}{x-3}\) to \(x+3\) and said the domain is all real numbers.

  1. What did the student do correctly?
  2. What domain detail did the student miss?
  3. Write the correct simplified expression and domain restriction.
6.

A fundraiser has revenue \(R(x)=12x+40\) and cost \(C(x)=8x+100\), where \(x\) is the number of items sold.

  1. Write a function for profit \(P(x)=R(x)-C(x)\).
  2. Interpret \(P(10)\) in context.
  3. Explain why subtracting functions makes sense in this situation.
7.

Explain the difference between simplifying a quotient of functions and changing the function itself.

Use \(\frac{x^2-1}{x-1}\) as your example. Mention excluded values, holes, or domain restrictions.

8.

Create two functions \(f\) and \(g\) so that:

  1. \((f+g)(x)\) is linear.
  2. \((fg)(x)\) is quadratic.
  3. \(\frac{f}{g}\) has an excluded value.

Explain why your choices satisfy all three conditions.
