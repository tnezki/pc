Unit 2.2 — Formative Spiral: Function Arithmetic
5.

True or False:

If \(g(x)=0\), then \(\frac{f(x)}{g(x)}\) is undefined.

Explain briefly.

Workspace
6.

Match each operation to its description:

  • sum
  • difference
  • product
  • quotient

Descriptions involve combined totals, comparisons, scaling, and remaining amounts.

Workspace
7.

Let \(f(x)=x^2-1\) and \(g(x)=2x+5\).

  1. Find \((f+g)(x)\).
  2. Find \((f-g)(x)\).
  3. Find \((fg)(x)\).
  4. Find \(\left(\frac{f}{g}\right)(x)\) and state its domain restriction.
Workspace
8.

The graph shown represents \(f(x)+g(x)\).

  1. Estimate \((f+g)(-2)\), \((f+g)(0)\), and \((f+g)(4)\).
  2. Explain how the sum graph relates to the values of \(f\) and \(g\).
Workspace
9.

Compare the functions \(f+g\), \(fg\), and \(\frac{f}{g}\). Use \(f(x)=x+1\) and \(g(x)=x-4\).

  1. Write each combined function.
  2. Compare their domains.
  3. Explain why the quotient requires more caution than the sum or product.
Workspace
10.

Use structure to determine whether each statement is always, sometimes, or never true.

  1. The domain of \(f+g\) is the intersection of the domains of \(f\) and \(g\).
  2. The domain of \(fg\) is the union of the domains of \(f\) and \(g\).
  3. The domain of \(\frac{f}{g}\) must exclude inputs where \(g(x)=0\).

Justify each answer.

Workspace
