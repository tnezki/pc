Unit A.1 Spiral — Transformations and Vertex Form of Quadratics
5.

For each function, identify the vertex and whether the vertex represents a maximum or minimum.

  1. \(y=(x+2)^2-9\)

  2. \(y=-3(x-1)^2+8\)

  3. \(y=\frac12(x-5)^2+4\)

  1. Identify the vertex.

  2. State the axis of symmetry.

  3. Does the vertex represent a maximum or minimum?

  4. Write a possible equation in vertex form.

7.

  1. \(y=(x+3)^2-1\)

  2. \(y=(x-3)^2+2\)

  3. \(y=3(x+1)^2\)

  4. \(y=x^2-3\)

8.

Describe the transformation from \(y=x^2\) to \(y=-(x+1)^2+4\). Use the words shift, reflect, and vertex in your answer.

9.

10.

  1. \(y=2(x+4)^2+1\)

  2. \(y=-2(x+4)^2+1\)

  3. \(y=-2(x-4)^2+1\)

  4. \(y=2(x-1)^2-4\)

11.

A parabola has vertex \((1,6)\) and passes through \((3,-2)\). A student reasons \(y=a(x+1)^2+6\), then uses \((3,-2)\) to find \(a=-\frac12\). Identify the error and solve correctly.

12.

A bridge arch is modeled by a quadratic function with vertex \((0,30)\). The arch touches the ground at \((-10,0)\) and \((10,0)\).

  1. Write the equation in vertex form.

  2. Explain why the graph must open downward.

  3. Find the height of the arch when \(x=5\).

  4. State the domain and range that make sense in context.

  5. Explain how the intercepts and vertex support your model.
