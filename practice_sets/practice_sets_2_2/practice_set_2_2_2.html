Unit 2.2 — Function Arithmetic
1

Let \(f(x)=x+5\) and \(g(x)=x-2\).

  1. Find \(\left(\frac{f}{g}\right)(x)\).
  2. State the value of \(x\) that must be excluded from the domain.
2

Let \(p(x)=x^2\) and \(q(x)=3x\).

  1. Find \((p+q)(4)\).
  2. Find \((p-q)(-2)\).
  3. Find \((pq)(1)\).
3

Let \(f(x)=x^2-1\) and \(g(x)=2x+5\).

  1. Find \((f+g)(x)\).
  2. Find \((f-g)(x)\).
  3. Find \((fg)(x)\).
  4. Find \(\left(\frac{f}{g}\right)(x)\) and state its domain restriction.
4

Let \(f(x)=x^2-4\) and \(g(x)=x-2\).

  1. Find \(\left(\frac{f}{g}\right)(x)\) and simplify.
  2. Explain why the simplified expression does not have the same domain as \(x+2\).
  3. Describe what would happen on a graph at the excluded input.

For \(x^{m/n}\), the denominator \(n\) tells the ______, and the numerator \(m\) tells the ______.

8

  1. \(\sqrt[3]{a^5}\)

  2. \(\sqrt{b^7}\)

  4. \(\sqrt[6]{n^5}\)

  1. \(b^{7/2}\)

  2. \(m^{3/4}\)

  3. \(a^{5/3}\)

  4. \(n^{5/6}\)
