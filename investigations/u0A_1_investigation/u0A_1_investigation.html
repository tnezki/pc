Precalculus Readiness - Unit A

Section A.1 Investigation

Transformations and Vertex Form of Quadratics

Investigation: What do the numbers in \(y=a(x-h)^2+k\) actually do?

Time: 15-20 minutes

Format: Desmos exploration, partner talk, and whole-class discussion

Learning Target: Analyze and graph quadratic functions in vertex form using transformations of the parent function.
Investigation Directions

Setup

  • Grouping: Partners or groups of 3.
  • Materials: Student handout, pencil, and one device per group.
  • Technology: Open Desmos Graphing Calculator.
  • Teacher display: Project Desmos or a blank coordinate grid for sharing discoveries.

Student Goal

Today you are not being told the rules first. Your job is to collect evidence from graphs and use that evidence to decide what \(a\), \(h\), and \(k\) do in vertex form.

\[y=a(x-h)^2+k\]

Desmos Start

  1. Go to desmos.com/calculator.
  2. Type: \(y=a(x-h)^2+k\)
  3. When Desmos asks, create sliders for \(a\), \(h\), and \(k\).
  4. Also type the parent function: \(y=x^2\).
  5. Use slider values that are easy to compare first: whole numbers between \(-5\) and \(5\).

Norms for the Investigation

  • Change only one slider at a time until the handout tells you otherwise.
  • Record what you see before writing a rule.
  • Use words like vertex, axis of symmetry, opens up/down, wider/narrower, shift, reflect, stretch, and compress.
  • Be ready to justify your rule with at least two examples.

Discussion Questions
  1. Why does \(y=(x-3)^2\) move right even though the equation contains \(x-3\)?
  2. How can you identify the vertex directly from \(y=a(x-h)^2+k\)?
  3. What does a negative \(a\)-value do that a positive \(a\)-value does not?
  4. How can you tell whether a parabola is vertically stretched or compressed?
  5. A student says \(y=-2(x+5)^2-1\) has vertex \((5,-1)\). What mistake did the student make?
Class Notes / Final Rule
Teaching Resource 1: Teacher Script

Purpose

This investigation is designed to let students discover the structure of vertex form before formal notes. Students should leave with an evidence-based rule for each parameter in \(y=a(x-h)^2+k\).

Launch: 2 minutes

Say: “Today your goal is to figure out what the numbers in vertex form do. Do not try to memorize first. Move one slider at a time, collect evidence, and then write a rule.”

Check: Every group should have \(y=x^2\) and \(y=a(x-h)^2+k\) entered in Desmos with sliders created.

Investigation: 10-12 minutes

  1. Part 1: Confirm that \(a=1\), \(h=0\), \(k=0\) matches \(y=x^2\).
  2. Part 2: Push students to use “up/down” and connect \(k\) to the y-coordinate of the vertex.
  3. Part 3: Pause groups if they say \(x+3\) means right 3. Ask, “What value of \(x\) makes the inside zero?”
  4. Part 4: Ask groups to compare \(|a|>1\) and \(0<|a|<1\). Do not accept only “changes shape”; ask how.
  5. Part 5: Require prediction before checking in Desmos.

Discussion: 5 minutes

Use the discussion questions to build the final class summary:

For \(y=a(x-h)^2+k\), the vertex is \((h,k)\), the axis of symmetry is \(x=h\), \(a\) controls opening direction and vertical stretch/compression, \(h\) shifts left/right, and \(k\) shifts up/down.

Teacher move: End by asking students to identify the vertex and transformations of \(y=-2(x+3)^2+4\) without graphing.

Common Misconceptions to Watch For

  • Thinking \((x-3)^2\) shifts left because of the minus sign.
  • Writing the vertex of \((x+5)^2-1\) as \((5,-1)\) instead of \((-5,-1)\).
  • Thinking any fraction makes the graph narrower. Fractions with absolute value less than 1 make the graph wider.
  • Describing \(a<0\) only as “negative” instead of “reflection over the \(x\)-axis / opens downward.”
Teaching Resource 2: Answer Key
Part 1: Build the Parent Function

Question: Set \(a=1\), \(h=0\), and \(k=0\). What do you notice?

Answer: The transformed equation becomes \(y=x^2\), so both graphs match.

Question: What is the vertex of the parent function?

Answer: \((0,0)\)

Question: What is the axis of symmetry?

Answer: \(x=0\)

Part 2: Investigate \(k\)

\(y=x^2-4\)\((0,-4)\)Shifted down 4
\(y=x^2+1\)\((0,1)\)Shifted up 1

Rule: \(k\) shifts the graph up or down and becomes the y-coordinate of the vertex.

Part 3: Investigate \(h\)

Rule: \(h\) shifts the graph left or right and becomes the x-coordinate of the vertex. The form is \(x-h\), so \(x+3\) means \(h=-3\).

Discussion Questions
  1. Why does \(y=(x-3)^2\) move right? The vertex occurs when \(x-3=0\), so \(x=3\). The graph shifts right 3.
  2. How can you identify the vertex? In \(y=a(x-h)^2+k\), the vertex is \((h,k)\).
  3. What does a negative \(a\)-value do? It reflects the parabola over the \(x\)-axis, so it opens downward.
  4. How can you tell stretch or compression? Use \(|a|\). If \(|a|>1\), the graph is narrower; if \(0<|a|<1\), it is wider.
  5. Student error: For \(y=-2(x+5)^2-1\), the vertex is \((-5,-1)\), not \((5,-1)\), because \(x+5=x-(-5)\).
