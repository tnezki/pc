Problem 5

A parabola has vertex \((3,7)\) and passes through the point \((5,15)\). Write the equation in vertex form.

Section A.1 — Speed Dating MathManistee High School
Solution 5

Start with vertex form using \((h,k)=(3,7)\):

\[y=a(x-3)^2+7\]

Substitute \((5,15)\):

\[15=a(5-3)^2+7\]
\[15=4a+7\]
\[a=2\]

Answer: \(y=2(x-3)^2+7\)

Problem 7

A parabola has vertex \((1,6)\) and passes through \((3,-2)\). A student reasons \(y=a(x+1)^2+6\), then uses \((3,-2)\) to find \(a=-\frac12\).

Identify the error and solve correctly.

Section A.1 — Speed Dating MathManistee High School
Solution 7

The error is using \((x+1)^2\). A vertex with \(h=1\) gives \((x-1)^2\).

\[y=a(x-1)^2+6\]

Use \((3,-2)\):

\[-2=a(3-1)^2+6\]
\[-2=4a+6\]
\[a=-2\]

Correct equation: \(y=-2(x-1)^2+6\)

Problem 8

A bridge arch is modeled by a quadratic function with vertex \((0,30)\). The arch touches the ground at \((-10,0)\) and \((10,0)\).

  1. Write the equation in vertex form.
  2. Explain why the graph must open downward.
  3. Find the height of the arch when \(x=5\).
  4. State the domain and range that make sense in context.
  5. Explain how the intercepts and vertex support your model.
Section A.1 — Speed Dating MathManistee High School
Solution 8

Use vertex form with vertex \((0,30)\): \(y=a(x-0)^2+30\).

Use \((10,0)\): \(0=100a+30\), so \(a=-\frac{3}{10}\).

Equation: \(y=-\frac{3}{10}x^2+30\)

  1. \(y=-\frac{3}{10}x^2+30\)
  2. It opens downward because the vertex is the highest point.
  3. \(y=-\frac{3}{10}(5)^2+30=22.5\)
  4. Domain: \(-10\le x\le 10\). Range: \(0\le y\le 30\).
  5. The vertex gives the maximum height, and the intercepts show where the arch reaches the ground.

Problem 16

A company models daily revenue using \(R(p)=-20(p-15)^2+5000\), where \(p\) is the price of one item in dollars.

  1. What price maximizes revenue?
  2. What is the maximum revenue?
  3. What is the revenue when \(p=10\)?
  4. Find another price that gives the same revenue as \(p=10\).
  5. Explain why two different prices can give the same revenue.
Section A.1 — Speed Dating MathManistee High School
Solution 16
  1. The price \(p=15\) maximizes revenue.
  2. The maximum revenue is \(5000\).
  3. \(R(10)=-20(10-15)^2+5000=4500\)
  4. Another price is \(p=20\), because it is also 5 units from 15.
  5. Quadratic functions are symmetric, so inputs the same distance from the axis of symmetry can have the same output.
