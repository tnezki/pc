Problem 1

For \(g(x)=-(x+1)^2-3\), identify the parent function and the transformations.

Section 1.4 — BrainstormingManistee High School
Solution 1

Parent function: \(y=x^2\).

Transformations: shift left 1, reflect over the \(x\)-axis, and shift down 3.

Problem 2

Section 1.4 — BrainstormingManistee High School
Solution 2

The dashed graph is the parent function \(y=x^2\).

The transformed graph has vertex \((-2,3)\) and opens downward, so a possible equation is:

\[y=-(x+2)^2+3\]

Problem 3

Two students describe \(g(x)=3f(x+2)-4\). Student A says the graph shifts left 2, stretches vertically by 3, and shifts down 4. Student B says the graph shifts right 2, stretches vertically by 3, and shifts down 4. Who is correct? Justify your answer.

Section 1.4 — BrainstormingManistee High School
Solution 3

Student A is correct.

In \(g(x)=3f(x+2)-4\), the input \(x+2\) shifts the graph left 2, the outside factor 3 creates a vertical stretch by 3, and the \(-4\) shifts the graph down 4.

Problem 9

A quadratic has \(x\)-intercepts at \((-4,0)\) and \((6,0)\), and passes through \((0,-24)\).

  1. Write the equation in intercept form.

  2. Rewrite the equation in standard form.

Section 1.4 — BrainstormingManistee High School
Solution 9
  1. Start with \(y=a(x+4)(x-6)\). Use \((0,-24)\): \(-24=a(4)(-6)\), so \(a=1\). Thus \(y=(x+4)(x-6)\).
  2. Standard form: \(y=x^2-2x-24\).

Problem 12

A quadratic function is shown by the table. \[ \begin{array}{c|ccccc} x&0&1&2&3&4\\ \hline f(x)&12&3&-2&-3&0 \end{array} \]

  1. Use first and second differences to verify the function is quadratic.

  2. Write the equation in standard form.

  3. Rewrite the equation in vertex form.

  4. Find the zeros using a method of your choice.

  5. Explain how the table, standard form, vertex form, and zeros all describe the same function.

Section 1.4 — BrainstormingManistee High School
Solution 12
  1. First differences: \(-9,-5,-1,3\). Second differences: \(4,4,4\), so the function is quadratic.
  2. Standard form: \(f(x)=2x^2-11x+12\).
  3. Vertex form: \(f(x)=2\left(x-\frac{11}{4}\right)^2-\frac{25}{8}\).
  4. Zeros: \(x=\frac{3}{2}\) and \(x=4\).
  5. The table shows the pattern, standard form shows the intercept, vertex form shows the turning point, and the zeros show where the graph crosses the \(x\)-axis.

Problem 16

A water tank is draining at a constant rate. The table shows the amount of water remaining.

Time (min)04812
Water (gal)90745842

  1. Write a linear equation for the amount of water remaining.

  2. Interpret the slope.

  3. Interpret the \(y\)-intercept.

  4. Determine when the tank will be empty.

  5. State a reasonable domain and range for the context.

Section 1.4 — BrainstormingManistee High School
Solution 16
  1. The equation is \(W=90-4t\).
  2. The slope means the tank loses 4 gallons per minute.
  3. The \(y\)-intercept means the tank starts with 90 gallons.
  4. The tank is empty when \(0=90-4t\), so \(t=22.5\) minutes.
  5. A reasonable domain is \(0\le t\le 22.5\). A reasonable range is \(0\le W\le 90\).
