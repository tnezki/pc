Problem 10

For the function:

$g(x)=3(x+1)(x-5)$

  1. Identify the zeros.
  2. Find the $y$-intercept.
  3. State the axis of symmetry.
  4. Explain how the zeros help you find the axis of symmetry.
Section 1.2 ‚Äî Whiteboard IndyManistee High School
Solution 10
  1. The zeros are $x=-1$ and $x=5$.
  2. $g(0)=3(1)(-5)=-15$, so the $y$-intercept is $(0,-15)$.
  3. The axis of symmetry is $x=2$.
  4. The axis is halfway between the zeros: $\frac{-1+5}{2}=2$.

Problem 12

A company models profit with $P(x)=-(x-4)(x-12)$, where $x$ is the number of items sold in hundreds and $P(x)$ is profit in thousands of dollars.

  1. What are the break-even points?
  2. What do the zeros mean in context?
  3. For what value of $x$ is profit maximized?
  4. What is the maximum profit?
  5. Explain why the model predicts positive profit only between the zeros.
Section 1.2 ‚Äî Whiteboard IndyManistee High School
Solution 12
  1. The break-even points occur at $x=4$ and $x=12$.
  2. The company makes $0$ profit when it sells $400$ or $1200$ items.
  3. The profit is maximized halfway between the zeros: $x=8$.
  4. $P(8)=-(8-4)(8-12)=16$, so the maximum profit is $16$ thousand dollars.
  5. The graph opens downward, so the function is above the $x$-axis between the zeros.
